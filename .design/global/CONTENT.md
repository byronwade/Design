# Global content and language

## Voice

Direct, specific, calm, and respectful. Prefer concrete domain language over vague product language. Describe what happened, what it affects, and what the person can do next.

## Labels

- use sentence case
- start actions with clear verbs
- name navigation for its destination, not a generic umbrella
- keep the same concept named the same way everywhere
- distinguish save, publish, submit, send, archive, remove, and delete
- avoid labels such as “OK,” “Done,” or “Continue” when the outcome can be named

## Status and errors

Do not report success before the system has the required evidence. Distinguish queued, processing, saved locally, synchronized, published, failed, stale, and offline. Error messages identify the problem, preserve entered data, and offer a next step.

## Empty and first-run states

Explain why the region is empty, whether the state is expected, and the most relevant next action. Do not fill empty states with generic illustration or multiple competing calls to action.

## Destructive language

Name the object and consequence. Avoid euphemism. State whether recovery, retention, or undo exists.

## Localization

Design for expansion, variable word order, right-to-left layout, pluralization, locale-specific dates and numbers, and text that does not fit the English prototype. Do not encode meaning in word position or icon direction alone.
